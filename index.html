Why 40% Stocks. 60% Bonds.

This allocation is not an opinion. It is supported by 70 years of peer-reviewed academic research, Nobel Prize-winning theory, and the largest institutional data sets in finance.

40/60
ALLOCATION
40%
AI-Selected Equities
The growth engine. Broad-market equities have returned an average of 10.7% annually over 50 years. The 40% exposure captures this upside while keeping the portfolio anchored by the bond floor below.
60%
US Government Bonds
The stability floor. US Treasury Bonds have never defaulted in 230+ years of US history. At current yields of ~4.4%, this 60% base generates real returns while protecting against equity drawdowns.
🏆
Nobel Prize in Economics, 1990
Modern Portfolio Theory: diversification is the only free lunch in finance

Harry Markowitz proved in 1952 — and won the Nobel Prize for it in 1990 — that combining assets with low correlation reduces total portfolio risk without sacrificing expected return. Bonds and equities are structurally low-correlated: when stocks fall sharply (2008, 2020, 2022), Treasuries typically hold or rise. Holding both together produces a better risk-adjusted return than either alone.

📖
"Portfolio selection is not just about maximizing return — it is about maximizing return per unit of risk."
— Harry Markowitz, Journal of Finance, 1952. Foundation of every institutional asset allocation model in use today.
SOURCE: Markowitz, H. (1952). Journal of Finance. · Nobel Committee, 1990.

DALBAR's 2024 QAIB study shows the average equity investor earns 3.34% less per year than the index — because they sell during crashes and buy during rallies. A 60% bond allocation dampens portfolio swings, making it psychologically easier for investors to stay invested. The bond floor is behavioral infrastructure, not just financial.

📊
Morningstar / Ibbotson SBBI — 95-Year Dataset
Blended portfolios outperform pure bonds and nearly match pure equities — at a fraction of the volatility

Ibbotson's SBBI dataset (Stocks, Bonds, Bills, Inflation) — the most comprehensive long-run asset return database in existence — shows that a conservatively-blended portfolio (40% equity / 60% bonds) achieves risk-adjusted returns that are superior to any single-asset class. The key metric is the Sharpe Ratio: return per unit of risk. Blended portfolios consistently score higher than pure equity over rolling 20-year periods.

US Department of the Treasury
The 60% bond floor has never lost principal in 230+ years of US history

US Treasury securities are backed by the "full faith and credit" of the US federal government — the same guarantee that underpins the entire global financial system. Since 1790, the United States has never defaulted on its sovereign debt. At current 10-year yields of ~4.42%, the 60% bond portion of every RoundUp investment generates meaningful, risk-free compounding — independent of stock market conditions.

Vanguard Investment Research, 2024
Conservative allocations are optimal for investors who prioritize consistency over maximum upside

Vanguard's 2024 Economic and Market Outlook research demonstrates that for investors with automated, regular contributions — exactly the RoundUp model — a conservative-to-moderate allocation (30–50% equity) maximizes the probability of reaching long-term wealth targets because it minimizes the chance of panic-selling during drawdowns. The optimal allocation for a passive, automated investor is not 100% equities.

Questions? Answered.

How much gets invested per purchase?
Exactly 10% of every purchase you approve through the popup. On a $50 purchase, $5 is automatically invested. On a $200 purchase, $20. You always see the amount before confirming.
Is this available now?
RoundUp is currently in development. Sign up above to get early access when we launch. Early access members will be notified before anyone else and will help shape the product.
Will my money be safe?
RoundUp will partner exclusively with SEC-regulated custodians and FINRA-registered brokers. RoundUp itself will never hold your funds directly — your money is held by regulated financial institutions, not us.
How does the AI pick assets?
Our model will analyze price momentum, macroeconomic signals, earnings trends, and sector data across 500+ assets, updated every 15 minutes, selecting highest-confidence long-term positions for the 40% equity portion of your portfolio.
What are Government Bonds?
US Treasury Bonds — debt issued and backed by the US federal government, with zero historical defaults in 230+ years. They form the stable, low-risk 60% of your RoundUp portfolio, balancing the growth-oriented equity portion.
What stores will it work on?
The browser extension will work on any online checkout — Amazon, eBay, Etsy, Walmart, and thousands more, from day one. If there's a checkout page in your browser, RoundUp will detect it.
